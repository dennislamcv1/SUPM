Scenario: Two-Day Seminar and Workshop

Project Scenario Success Inc. is an investment company with offices throughout the United States and Canada. Their primary goal is to provide personalized asset management and investment guidance to individuals with portfolios exceeding US $250,000. Success hosts a twice-yearly two-day seminar and workshop for potential investors. Day 1 of the seminar is for Success Inc. executive employees. This initial day is for sharing information, providing an environment for the team to network, and preparing for day 2. Day 2 is for all potential investors. Investors can attend in person. Success Inc. will stream presentations for those who cannot attend. Success Inc. will host its fall event in Dallas, Texas, on March 31, 2024.

Juan Cortez is the Project Manager. The Sponsor for the project is his manager, Sandra Morgan. They are both assigned to the Success Inc. Operations branch. The final project plan includes the following requirements:

  • The event location is the Gateway Hotel and Convention Center in Dallas. Success Inc. distributed invitations to approximately 350 clients to attend. Success Inc. invited another 200 potential clients to participate in the virtual option. The event anticipates parking costs for approximately 175 vehicles.

  • Comprehensive investment packets defining the service offerings and gift packets must be assembled and distributed to all attendees. Those attending in person will receive their packets at the event at check-in. Those people virtually attending must receive their packets via express mail.

  • The event requires two days of catering. Day 1 will host approximately 50 people. Day 2 will host those 50 individuals and 350 potential clients for a total of 400 meals. Catering includes a continental breakfast, box lunches, non-alcoholic hot and cold beverages, and light snacks throughout the day. A comprehensive catering plan was developed and approved.

  • The hotel will handle conference area setup and shutdown. Juan needs to guide the hotel staff. There will be charges for these services.

  • The company provides funding. Juan will be required to provide finance with an itemized list of costs at the end of the event for invoicing. An initial budget for the project was developed and approved. This overview includes financial estimates.

  • The team must coordinate all speakers, manage the agenda, address developing issues, and ensure the program runs smoothly. An external motivational speaker is required.

  • The event requires extensive audiovisual (AV) and Information Management IT support. The internal teams can provide support; however, the event might require additional outsourced support. Success Inc.'s IT Department is evaluating requirements.

  • Sandra requested weekly status reports and a twice-weekly status meeting and established a Change Control Board consisting of herself and all Success Inc. Department leads.

The Project to Date

  • The leaders approved the project plan on December 30. Project planning began on December 1. It is currently January 31. The event will occur in two months. The project has been relatively smooth. But there are some issues. The total project duration is four months or 17 weeks in all.

  • Priya hoped to have replies for 70% of the invitations by now. Only 50% are received. The Dallas area has been hit hard by the flu this year. She is hoping that this is not a detractor. In addition, the current economic conditions are unfavorable due to inflation and a tightening job market.

  • Ramesh worked under Sarah's guidance to develop a catering plan and finalize proposal reviews and vendor selection schedules. Sarah will lead the source selection and finalize contracts for all external support requirements. One vendor will satisfy the budget constraints perfectly. However, this vendor does not have gluten-free offerings. A second proposal will exceed the budget by ten percent. The good news is that they can satisfy all dietary restrictions. The team informed Sandra Morgan of the issue; her decision is pending.

  • Sophia is working with the Success Inc. IT team, who might be unable to support some streaming requirements and need external support. The IT lead promised to provide Sophia with an answer in one week.

  • The team accelerated their project planning, but some key planning documents were incomplete. The project planning includes a team RACI, a Communications Management Plan, and a Resource Breakdown Structure.

  • Sandra is finding it difficult to identify a motivational speaker who satisfies the CEO's budget constraints and subject matter requirements, which is an issue that the team must address before February 10.
